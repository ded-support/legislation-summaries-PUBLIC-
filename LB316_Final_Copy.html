Summary

Legislative Bill 316 primarily addresses the regulation and taxation of cannabidiol products. It specifies that beginning January 1, 2026, a 10% excise tax will be applied to the retail sale of cannabidiol products. Retail sellers are required to maintain electronic sales records and submit regular tax returns and payments to the Department of Revenue. These payments are credited to the Property Tax Credit Cash Fund. The bill stipulates that the Department of Revenue is responsible for tax collection and reporting. Additionally, cannabidiol products are explicitly defined within the context of the bill, aligning them with existing state and federal laws regarding controlled substances and hemp regulations.

Underlined (Added) Text Summary

The legislative bill introduces an excise tax on the retail sale of cannabidiol products starting January 1, 2026.
