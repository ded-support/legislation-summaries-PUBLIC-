Summary

Legislative Bill 258 requires the Nebraska Department of Labor to calculate and publish the updated minimum wage rate annually, starting from October 15, 2026, to take effect on January 1 of the following year. This task involves determining increments for minimum wage on an annual basis as outlined in the legislation, starting January 1, 2027, with a stipulated increase of one and three-quarters percent. No changes to funding, appropriations, or budget allocations for the Department of Labor are explicitly detailed in the bill.

Underlined (Added) Text Summary

The underlined legislative text introduces provisions concerning wages for certain youth employees. Specifically, it establishes a youth minimum wage of $13.50 per hour for employees between 14 and 15 years old who are not emancipated minors. Starting January 1, 2030, this wage will increase by 1.5% every five years, adjusted to the nearest cent. Additionally, a training wage rate is specified at $13.50 per hour until December 31, 2026. From January 1, 2027, the training wage will increase annually by 1.5%, also rounded to the nearest cent.
