Legislative Bill 258, introduced in the Nebraska Legislature, involves amendments to the state's Wage and Hour Act. It mandates annual minimum wage adjustments based on specific percentages. The Department of Labor is tasked with calculating and publishing the wage rate adjustments no later than October 15 of each year, beginning in 2026. This publication will affect the wage implementation for the following January. The bill also outlines provisions for a youth minimum wage and a training wage rate, providing employers the ability to pay reduced rates under specified conditions. Underlined (Added) Text Summary

The legislative bill outlines provisions for youth and training minimum wages. For the youth minimum wage, employers may pay $13.50 per hour to employees aged 14 to 15 who are not emancipated. Starting January 1, 2030, this rate will increase by 1.5% every five years, rounded to the nearest cent. For the training wage, the rate is set at $13.50 per hour until December 31, 2026. From January 1, 2027, it will increase annually by 1.5%, also rounded to the nearest cent.
