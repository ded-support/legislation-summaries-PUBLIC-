Summary

Legislative Bill 258 introduces changes to the minimum wage regulations within Nebraska and includes specific directives for the Nebraska Department of Labor. The bill adjusts the minimum wage incrementally, reaching $15 per hour by January 1, 2026, and mandates annual increases starting in 2027 by 1.75%. The Nebraska Department of Labor is tasked with calculating and publishing these adjustments by October 15 each year, commencing in 2026. There are no explicit changes to funding, appropriations, or budget allocations concerning the Department of Labor mentioned in the bill.

Underlined (Added) Text Summary

The legislative additions specify wage adjustments affecting youth and training wages. Employers may pay a youth minimum wage of $13.50 per hour to employees aged 14 to 15 who are not emancipated minors. Starting January 1, 2030, this youth minimum wage will increase by 1.5% every five years, rounded to the nearest cent. Additionally, the training wage rate is set at $13.50 per hour until December 31, 2026. From January 1, 2027, the training wage will also increase annually by 1.5%, rounded to the nearest cent. There are no specified changes to funding, appropriations, or budget allocations for the Department of Labor in the provided text.
