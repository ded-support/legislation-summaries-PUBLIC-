Summary

Legislative Bill 258, from the Nebraska Legislature, mandates the Nebraska Department of Labor to undertake specific responsibilities regarding the minimum wage. By October 15 each year, starting in 2026, the Department must calculate and publish the adjusted minimum wage rate. This adjustment will be based on a one and three-quarters percent increase and will take effect the following January 1. The bill does not mention changes to funding, appropriations, or budget allocations for the Department of Labor. The focus is instead on procedural duties related to the annual adjustment and publication of wage rates.

Underlined (Added) Text Summary

The legislative bill introduces specific provisions for youth and training minimum wage rates. Employers can pay a youth minimum wage of $13.50 per hour to employees aged 14 to 15 who are not emancipated, with this rate set to increase by 1.5% every five years starting January 1, 2030. Additionally, a training wage rate of $13.50 per hour is established, effective until December 31, 2026. Beginning January 1, 2027, this training wage rate will increase annually by 1.5%. There are no specific changes to funding, appropriations, or budget allocations for the Department of Labor mentioned in this text.
