Summary

Legislative Bill 34 proposes changes to the time standards in Nebraska. The bill provides for year-round daylight saving time, contingent on federal authorization and similar legislative changes in three adjacent states. It amends existing statutes regarding the standard time, establishing it as Coordinated Universal Time minus five hours for the central time zone and minus six hours for the mountain time zone. The bill involves coordination with the United States Secretary of Commerce and the Secretary of Transportation.
