Summary

Legislative Bill 135 amends various sections of the Nebraska statutes to update processes involving elections, with a focus on propositions related to school district funding and governance. The bill involves changes impacting the conduct of elections under the purview of the Government, Military and Veterans Affairs Committee. Key provisions include clarifications and modifications in the procedures for submitting election issues by political subdivisions, particularly school districts, which must comply with new notice requirements and election arrangements as outlined in subsection (3) of section 32-559. It outlines the processes and conditions under which school districts can propose bond issues, alterations in tax levies, or budget expansions to voters. It mandates that such propositions are subject to a majority vote by qualified electors during elections, which could occur at primary, general, or special elections. Importantly, the bill also repeals section 10-703.01, eliminating certain statutory content.

Underlined (Added) Text Summary

The legislative bill outlines the procedures for school district elections regarding issuance of bonds, property tax levies, or exceeding levy limitations. It specifies that such elections should follow subdivision (b) or (c) requirements and cannot be held within ten months of a failed attempt. Registered voters determine election outcomes by majority vote.

Key details include:

- Elections can coincide with statewide primary, general elections, or odd-year elections of political subdivisions.

- School boards must file election orders or certify ballot questions by specific deadlines.

- Notices are mailed 30 days before elections, and sample ballots are published 7 to 15 days prior.

- Elections are managed by the election commissioner or county clerk, with two disinterested persons assisting in ballot counting.

- Procedures for early voting ballots and voter certifications include inter-county cooperation if school districts span multiple counties.

- Election workers receive wages at least equal to the minimum rate specified.
