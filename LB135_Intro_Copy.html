Summary

Legislative Bill 135 amends various sections of Nebraska statutes concerning election-related procedures, impacting several entities including school districts, governmental units, and joint entities. Key changes include specifying the process for issuing bonds by school districts and joint entities, which now require voter approval at an election following Section 32-559(3) procedures. The bill clarifies budget limitations for governmental units, with provisions for exceeding these through voter approval. It outlines the allocation and levy authority related to joint public agencies and modifies election timing and procedures for ballot measures involving school districts and other political subdivisions.

Agencies notably mentioned include:

- School Districts: Subject to voter approval for bond issuances and budgetary overrides.

- Educational Service Units: Have provisions for exceeding budget limitations.

- Election Commissioners/County Clerks: Tasked with managing election outcomes and certifications.

No specific changes to appropriations or new budget allocations are detailed in the provided text.

Underlined (Added) Text Summary

The legislative additions specify election procedures for school district-related matters, such as bond issuance, property tax levy approval, or exceeding tax levy limits. Elections must comply with subsection (3) of section 32-559, and generally occur during statewide primary or general elections, or in odd-numbered years during regular political subdivision elections. The school board is responsible for filing the ballot order and certifying questions by set deadlines: March 1 for primaries and September 1 for general elections. County election commissioners or clerks conduct these elections, handle early voting arrangements, and count ballots with appointed disinterested persons. They must provide notice of elections and publish sample ballots in local newspapers. Furthermore, no funding, appropriations, or budget allocations are mentioned in the added provisions.
